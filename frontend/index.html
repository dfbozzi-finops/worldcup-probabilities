METHODOLOGY & ARCHITECTURE

1. Dixon-Coles Bivariate Poisson Model: The core engine utilizes a time-decayed bivariate Poisson regression model to calculate team attacking and defensive strengths. This allows us to simulate exact goal expectancies for any matchup.

2. MCMC Simulation (PyMC): We use Markov Chain Monte Carlo to simulate the entire tournament trajectory 10,000 times, providing robust probability distributions for complex futures (e.g., "To Reach Semi-Finals").

3. Player Props (Stochastic Engine): Player-level metrics are scraped from FBref and passed through fractional allocation and Poisson/Negative Binomial distributions to generate actionable 'Over/Under' probabilities for Shots on Target and Assists.

4. Expected Value (EV): Our edge is calculated as EV = (True Probability × Implied Market Odds) - 1. Only positive EV opportunities are recommended for execution.

5. Kelly Criterion: Capital allocation is dynamically sized using the fractional Kelly Criterion to maximize long-term bankroll growth while strictly minimizing ruin risk.
